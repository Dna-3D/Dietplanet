5 Healthy Study Snacks Every FUTO Student Should Try

December 15, 2024 | Diet Planet Team

Studying can be exhausting, and it's easy to reach for unhealthy snacks. Here are five nutritious options that will keep your energy levels stable and your mind sharp during those long study sessions.

1. Mixed Nuts and Dried Fruits

Perfect combination of healthy fats, protein, and natural sugars to fuel your brain.

2. Greek Yogurt with Berries

Rich in protein and antioxidants, this snack supports concentration and memory.

3. Whole Grain Crackers with Avocado

Provides sustained energy and healthy fats that support brain function.

4. Dark Chocolate (70% or higher)

Contains flavonoids that can improve cognitive function and mood.

5. Fresh Fruit Smoothie

Hydrating and packed with vitamins to keep you alert and focused.

Budget-Friendly Meal Planning for University Students

December 10, 2024 | Diet Planet Team

Managing food expenses while maintaining a healthy diet can be challenging for students. Here's how to eat well without breaking the bank.

Weekly Meal Planning Tips:
  • Plan your meals in advance to avoid impulse purchases
  • Buy ingredients that can be used in multiple meals
  • Take advantage of student discounts and bulk pricing
  • Cook in batches and store portions for later
  • Balance convenience with nutrition
Budget-Friendly Protein Sources:
  • Eggs - versatile and affordable
  • Beans and lentils - plant-based protein
  • Chicken - cost-effective animal protein
  • Tuna - convenient and nutritious

The Importance of Hydration During Exam Season

December 5, 2024 | Diet Planet Team

During intense study periods, students often forget to stay properly hydrated. Learn why hydration is crucial for academic performance and how to maintain it.

Why Hydration Matters:
  • Improves concentration and memory
  • Prevents fatigue and headaches
  • Maintains optimal brain function
  • Supports overall health and immunity
Hydration Tips:
  • Keep a water bottle at your study desk
  • Set reminders to drink water regularly
  • Include hydrating foods like fruits and vegetables
  • Limit caffeine and sugary drinks
  • Start each day with a glass of water
